## The Challenge of Stigma

PWDs in Uganda face significant stigma and discrimination. Many are excluded from education, employment, and community participation due to misconceptions and negative attitudes. At JOSSUD, we believe that sustainable change requires shifting mindsets at the community level.

## Our Advocacy Approach

JOSSUD works on multiple fronts to promote disability inclusion and challenge stigma:

### Community Awareness Campaigns

We conduct targeted awareness campaigns in markets, schools, churches, and community gatherings. These campaigns address:

- Myths and misconceptions about disabilities
- The capabilities and potential of PWDs
- Disability rights and legal protections
- Inclusive practices for businesses and institutions

### Youth Leadership Programs

We engage young people as ambassadors for disability inclusion. Our youth leaders:

- Conduct peer-to-peer education
- Use social media to spread awareness
- Organize school-based disability inclusion programs
- Mentor other young PWDs

### Policy Engagement

JOSSUD advocates at the local government level for:

- Disability-inclusive policies and budgets
- Enforcement of disability laws
- Accessible public services
- PWD participation in decision-making

## Impact Stories

### Schools Becoming Inclusive

Through our partnerships with schools, we've trained teachers on inclusive education. Result: 15 schools have improved their enrollment and retention of PWD students.

### Businesses Becoming Accessible

Our business advocacy program has worked with 30+ businesses to improve accessibility and employment of PWDs. Over 50 PWDs have gained employment through these partnerships.

### Communities Embracing Inclusion

In three pilot communities, we've seen a marked shift in attitudes. PWD participation in community meetings has increased by 40%, and many PWDs now hold community leadership positions.

## The Power of Peer Advocacy

One of our most powerful tools is peer advocacy—when PWDs themselves become advocates for change. Our trained PWD advocates are:

- Speaking at community events
- Sharing their stories
- Mentoring other PWDs
- Influencing their families and friends

## What's Next?

We're expanding our advocacy reach to:

- **10 more communities** in 2024
- **100 new youth ambassadors** to be trained
- **Stronger partnerships** with media outlets for sustained campaigns
- **Policy advocacy** at the district and national levels

- Speak up against discrimination
- Support PWD-led initiatives
- Advocate for inclusion in your workplace or school

Together, we can build a Uganda where disability inclusion is the norm, not the exception.
