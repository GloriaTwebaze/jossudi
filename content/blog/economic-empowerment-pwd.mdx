## Breaking the Cycle of Poverty

Persons with Disabilities in Uganda often face unique economic challenges. Limited access to formal financial institutions, discrimination from lenders, and lack of collateral create barriers to economic participation. JOSUDDI's Village Savings and Loan Associations (VSLAs) are changing this narrative.

### What is a VSLA?

A VSLA is a community-based financial group where members save money together and access loans from their collective pool. Instead of going to banks, PWDs in our VSLAs:

- Save money safely without banking fees
- Access emergency loans when needed
- Build credit histories
- Learn financial management
- Support each other as peers

### Real Impact

Over the past two years, our VSLA members have collectively saved more than **UGX 50 million**. This capital has been used for:

- Healthcare and disability-related expenses
- Education for children
- Starting income-generating businesses
- Home improvements
- Emergency situations

## The VSLA Model in Action

Our groups meet regularly to:

1. **Save Together** - Members contribute fixed amounts weekly or monthly
2. **Learn Together** - Financial literacy and business training sessions
3. **Lend Together** - Members access loans at reasonable rates
4. **Grow Together** - Dividends are distributed from profits

### Success Story

*Grace's Journey:* Grace, a wheelchair user, saved UGX 15,000 monthly through her VSLA. After six months, she accessed a UGX 2 million loan to start a small shop. Today, her business generates UGX 2 million monthly, and she employs another PWD.

## Beyond Money

VSLAs offer more than financial benefits:

- **Social Inclusion** - PWDs are part of a supportive community
- **Dignity** - Economic independence restores self-worth
- **Leadership** - PWDs take leadership roles in groups
- **Network** - Access to peer support and mentorship
